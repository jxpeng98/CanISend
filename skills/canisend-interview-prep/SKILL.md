# CanISend Interview Prep

Focus only on interview preparation after or alongside an application. Do not invent answers, experiences, or employer knowledge.

## Boundaries

- Do not submit applications, fill portals, create accounts, upload materials, or answer sensitive declarations.
- Do not fabricate applicant evidence, interview outcomes, employer preferences, panel knowledge, examples, metrics, or availability.
- Ask before reading full private CVs, statements, references, full job adverts, source URLs, PDFs, or enabling LLM-backed commands.
- Prefer generated evidence under `profile/generated/`, `parsed_job.json`, criteria checklists, cover letters, statements, and material review notes over raw private files.
- Treat weak examples as practice gaps, not as invitations to embellish.

## Required References

Read only what the current task requires:

- `../canisend/references/privacy.md`: private-material handling and quoting limits.
- `../canisend/references/quality-gates.md`: evidence and consistency gates.
- `../canisend/references/file-contracts.md`: job, evidence, draft, and review artifact paths.
- `../canisend/references/workflow.md`: local-first generation sequence.

## Workflow

1. Identify the interview format: screening call, academic panel, teaching demo, research talk, professional interview, or informal conversation.
2. Prefer `parsed_job.json`, `05_criteria_checklist.md`, `02_fit_report.md`, submitted drafts, and `profile/generated/` evidence.
3. Convert role criteria into likely questions and evidence-backed answer themes.
4. Build answers using a clear structure: situation, evidence, relevance to the role, and concise takeaway.
5. Prepare gaps honestly: what to acknowledge, how to redirect to adjacent evidence, and what not to claim.
6. Draft questions for the panel that show role understanding without pretending insider knowledge.
7. Before saying interview prep is ready, check `../canisend/references/quality-gates.md`.

## Output

Return:

- Likely interview questions
- Evidence-backed answer bullets
- Risks or weak examples to prepare
- Questions to ask the panel
- Follow-up note draft when requested
